Map

Jordan is situated in the Middle East, bordered by Syria to the north, Iraq to the northeast, Saudi Arabia to the east and south, and Israel and Palestine to the west. The map would show key cities like Amman (the capital), Aqaba, and Petra, along with major road networks, the Dead Sea, and geographic landmarks like Wadi Rum.

ABOUT

THOBE

The Jordanian thobe is a traditional long dress adorned with intricate embroidery, reflecting Jordan’s rich cultural heritage. Each region has unique patterns and colors, symbolizing local traditions and history.

MUSIC

Jordanian music combines traditional Arabic sounds with modern influences, featuring instruments like the oud and tabla. Folk styles like dabke celebrate heritage, while newer genres add a contemporary edge.

FOOD

Jordanian cuisine is rich and flavorful, with famous dishes like mansaf (lamb with rice and yogurt sauce), maqluba (layered rice and meat), and kofta. It blends Bedouin traditions with Mediterranean flavors, emphasizing hospitality and sharing.

PETRA

Petra, the “Rose City” of Jordan, is an ancient city carved into pink sandstone, known for sites like the Treasury. It's a UNESCO World Heritage site and a Wonder of the World.

CITY

Traditional Jordanian houses are built close together because families enjoy living in communities. This design fosters strong social bonds and encourages gatherings among neighbors.

WEDDINGS

Jordanian weddings are lively celebrations lasting several days, featuring traditional music, dance, and elaborate feasts, with the bride in an embroidered dress and the groom in formal attire.

Delicious Food

Mansaf is Jordan’s national dish made of lamb in yogurt sauce served over rice, often garnished with nuts. It symbolizes hospitality and is popular at special occasions.
